Things I will do.

Similar to my Nope list I present a list of "Things I will do."

WILL I...

Knowledge & Information

  • ...encourage information to be free and accessible.
  • ...strongly encourage education.
  • ...read widely and across disciplines.
  • ...write long-form essays and publish them freely.
  • ...share my research publicly even when incomplete.
  • ...value primary sources over summaries.
  • ...maintain a personal library (physical and digital).
  • ...teach what I learn.

Computing & Technology

  • ...use free and open-source software wherever possible.
  • ...run Linux as my primary operating system.
  • ...patch my systems.
  • ...self-host services rather than rely on third parties.
  • ...build my own tools when existing ones fall short.
  • ...prefer simple software over feature-bloated alternatives.
  • ...encrypt my communications.
  • ...own my own data.
  • ...use a tiling window manager.
  • ...maintain a homelab.
  • ...read source code before trusting software.
  • ...contribute to open-source projects.

Work & Craft

  • ...focus on one thing at a time rather than multitask.
  • ...stay organized with systems (kanban, task tracking).
  • ...learn through doing (workbooks over textbooks).
  • ...drill fundamentals relentlessly.
  • ...build foundations before chasing advanced topics.
  • ...value depth over breadth in any given session.
  • ...write every day.
  • ...treat my website as a living document.
  • ...track what I consume (books, film, music, anime).
  • ...version control everything.

Ethics & Character

  • ...treat people based solely on their actions.
  • ...hold myself to an absolutist ethical standard.
  • ...stick with time-tested moral priors over fashionable arguments.
  • ...give respect and kindness freely by default (but revoke it when warranted).
  • ...tell people they are appreciated.
  • ...increase my bank of patience.
  • ...become positive by default.
  • ...foster a safe and welcoming space wherever I am.
  • ...admit when I am wrong.
  • ...hold strong opinions loosely where evidence demands it.

Personal

  • ...value experiences over material possessions.
  • ...minimize possessions that do not serve a purpose.
  • ...make time for wasting time.
  • ...take care of my physical health.
  • ...avoid social media.
  • ...protect my attention as a finite resource.
  • ...choose quality over convenience.
  • ...build things that outlast me.
  • ...care about form as much as function.
  • ...fund my own work rather than depend on institutions.

Faith & Philosophy

  • ...coexist as both scientist and believer.
  • ...practice subtraction rather than addition in my life.
  • ...resist epistemic learned helplessness.
  • ...maintain moral dogma where it has proven good.
  • ...think in decades, not days.
